League Constitution

1. League Overview Platform: Fantrax Format: Daily Lineups, Weekly Head‑to‑Head Matchups Scoring System: Fangraphs Points https://ottoneu.fangraphs.com/scoringoptions Teams: 12 Please take the time to review Fantrax League Page for base settings and rules 2. Finances 2.1 Buy-In & Security Deposit Annual Buy-In: $20 First year $20 buy in and NO deposit! First year is a trial run year with only 5 keepers to test league settings and allow owners to see if they enjoy the format before being locked in multiple seasons One‑time $40 security deposit collected after Year 1 to avoid “Hit & Run” owners Refunded over five seasons: No refund after Season 2 $10 refunded after each season (Seasons 3–6) 2.3 Fund Management All funds held through LeagueSafe Majority‑vote approval required for all payouts Commissioner cannot withdraw funds without league approval 2.4 Prizes Playoffs 1st: $120 2nd: $60 3rd: $30 Regular Season Most Points Overall (not necessarily best H2H W/L record): $30 3. Roster Structure 3.1 MLB Roster (33 Players) 22 Starters: C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, MI, OF × 5, UTIL SP × 5, RP × 5 11 Reserve Spots Reserve slots aren't limited in Fantrax, as long as owners don't exceed 33 total MLB players. For example, sitting extra SP's in reserves to strategically fill their 8 GS for the week 3.2 Minor‑League Roster (7 Players) Players must follow Fantrax definition of minor league player when acquiring Prospects may only be added via draft or trade (no in‑season FA adds to minors) When players are called up to MLB in real life, they may be held in your minor league roster until the end of that season (this could potentially happen multiple times if players moves up and down in real life). Must move to MLB roster in the offseason if not moved in-season. This means an owner may strategically wait until after the midseason cutoff to move a player to their MLB roster to avoid a year of service time logged. After being moved to your MLB roster, they will be subject to keeper rules as outlined below If a player is moved up to MLB and then moved down to minors in real life, owners may move them back down into their minors roster as long as Fantrax adds the minor league flag. 3.3 Injured Reserve (IR) (3 Players) Players may be placed in these slots if on the real life MLB IL These 3 slots do not count towards the overall roster size limitations When players come off the real life MLB IL, teams must remove them from the IR slot within the 1 day grace period If not removed by this time, transactions (adds/drops/trades/lineup moves) will be blocked and owner may be disqualified from H2H matchups at commissioner's discretion Owners may add players directly to their IL during the season 4. Keeper Rules 4.1 MLB Keepers Each team may keep 22 MLB players each offseason (ONLY 5 MLB/MiLB KEEPERS AFTER SEASON 1!) MLB keepers include any player on the 33‑man roster except prospects still in MiLB slots 2-Way players, such as Ohtani, will count as 2 keepers each 4.2 MiLB Keepers Unlimited MiLB keepers with no service time limit (Does not apply to season 1) 4.3 Franchise Players (NOT APPLICABLE TO SEASON 1 KEEPER COUNT OF 5 ONLY) Each Team may have 2 MLB Keepers that can exceed the 3 year service time rules: 1 Franchise Hitter 1 Franchise Pitcher Franchise players have unlimited keep time (service time rule below does not apply) 4.4 Service‑Time Limit (Includes Season 1->2 Keepers) All non‑franchise MLB players may be kept for a maximum of 3 seasons of service time A player who reaches 3 seasons must be cut or traded in the following offseason before the keeper deadline 4.5 Trading Keepers with Existing Service Time To recreate real life playoff push and rebuild cycles, service time will transfer with traded players. This will create scenarios for example where a top team would trade away a promising prospect or player with low service time to a rebuilding team for their star outfielder on his 3rd year of service time. Trading a player will transfer their existing service time from the previous team. Only drafted or free agent add players will start with zero service time on your team. Franchise Player Trades: When acquiring a player with > 3 years service time (franchise player) during the season, that owner may roster that player until the end of the season. During the next offseason, they must drop the player or elect to use one of their franchise keeper slots to keep the player at > 3 years service time. When acquiring a player with > 3 years service time (franchise player) during the offseason, that owner must use one of their franchise keeper slots to roster the player for the upcoming season (or drop/trade away prior to season start) 5. Service‑Time Calculation Service time is based on when a player is acquired or activated: Full Season Anyone on your MLB roster before the half‑season cutoff (June 29, 2026; 2 weeks prior to trade deadline each season) will accrue 1 full season of service time A full season of service time will apply even if you drop and re-add the player later from free agency during the same season Not Full Season Anyone acquired or activated on or after the half‑season cutoff (June 29, 2026; 2 weeks prior to trade deadline each season) will not accrue service time that season Exception: Acquiring players via trade will transfer existing service time Players in your minor league do not accrue service time If a minor league player is moved into your MLB roster and back down to Minors in the same season, regardless of timing, service time will be treated as a full season if in YOUR MLB roster >= 80 days that season (days don't roll over, treated on a season-by-season basis) 6. Player Acquisition Rules Free‑agent prospects marked as "Minors" by Fantrax cannot be added to Minors slots; MLB roster only (manually enforced rule) Free agents are automatically put on waivers at the start of the first game each week (Mondays) Waivers are processed at 10pm Central Time each Sunday evening Max of 3 free agent claims per week Add/drops are locked at the beginning of the first game of the playoffs and won't re-open until after the offseason draft An owner may request a mid-matchup add to their roster not count towards the 3 claims per week limit for a player being moved to the IL (or a player being dropped if they are on real life IL). The commissioner would need to manually perform the 4th add and will check waiver priority if required for the 4th+ claim. 7. Schedule / Misc 21 Scoring Periods Regular Season (Play each opponent twice, except matchup 1 opponent) Midseason service time cutoff date: June 29, 2026 (Players acquired or called up on/after this date don't accrue a year of service time for the current season) Trade Deadline: July 12, 2026 (Last day for trades) Adds/Drops Deadline: August 30, 2026 Playoffs: August 31 - September 20, 2026 August 31 - September 6: Semifinals September 7-20: Finals & Consolation Matchup for 3rd Place Offseason Trading Opens: September 21, 2026 Pitcher Limitations: Maximum of 8 GS for SP and RP slots combined for each weekly matchup (Prorated for matchups longer than 7 days) IF GS LIMIT EXCEEDED ON THE SAME DAY, FANTRAX WILL NOT AUTOMATICALLY ZERO OUT SP STATS - COMMISSIONER MUST MANUALLY REMOVE STATS FOR 9TH+ GS. OWNER MUST NOTIFY LEAGUE VIA FANTRAX CHAT AND/OR COMMISSIONER BEFORE GAMES START, OTHERWISE COMMISSIONER WILL AUTOMATICALLY COUNT ONLY THE FIRST PLAYED GAME (BASED ON START TIME). IF START TIME FOR 8TH & 9TH GAMES ARE THE SAME, COMMISSIONER WILL USE SP THAT HAD ACCUMULATED THE MOST POINTS PER GAME YTD, INCLUDING THAT DAY'S PERFORMANCE. Any player pitching out of the SP slot OR a pitcher following an opener with the intent of pitching 3+ innings (regardless if in SP or RP slot) will be manually charged a GS stat retroactively by the commissioner, regardless if they started the game or not. Owners should report known instances (most commonly an SP following an opener) to the commissioner, as this is manually enforced and corrected. If a pitcher is used in an "Opener" role (out of the RP slot only), owners may request it does not count towards their 8 GS matchup limit. The commissioner will not actively check if pitchers were used in this non-traditional GS, so if it is not reported by the owner and they exceed 8 GS that week, it's likely the commissioner will not take the exception without the owner's request. Likewise, the commissioner will not retroactively change the results of a matchup more than 24 hours after the conclusion (by 11pm Central Time each Monday). It's the owner's responsibility to request the exception, and the commissioner's discretion to grant it. If there is doubt that a pitcher was not intending on being limited to 3 or fewer innings, the commissioner won't grant an exception. If a pitcher was 'intended' on being a short term opener but pitches 3+ innings, it will count towards a GS regardless. Due to the manual enforcement of these rules, owners are encouraged to report observed violations to the commissioner. If a violation is discovered after the conclusion of a matchup, commissioner reserves the right to retroactively correct the stats and (potentially) outcome of the matchup as a result. 8. Offseason Schedule Adds/Drops are locked (Locked since playoffs began previous season) Trades are Open for All following the last playoff day Keeper Deadline (Feb 1) Slow Draft Starts (March 1) 9. Draft and Competitive Balance 9.1 Draft Order Draft order for the annual offseason draft: 4th-12th in order then Playoff Finishes: 3rd, 2nd, 1st 9.2 Competitive Balance Adjustment STARTING AFTER YEAR 2 ONLY: Teams finishing 10th, 11th, and 12th receive 2 extra MLB keeper slots for the following offseason only 10. Record Keeping Owners can access their players' service time records via Fantrax menu. Commissioner will keep a log of keepers and service time, updated each offseason following the keeper deadline. This log will be shared with league members at a central location. 11. Violations Owners violating rules or engaging in collusion may be removed or disqualified from prizes by the commissioner at his descretion, without refund of league dues and security deposit. In-season violations and disputes not clearly outlined in these rules will be resolved by the commissioner in the spirit of competitive integrity and fairness.
