Official Midseason Report Weeks 1-11 Recap

Stormy Seas & Glistening Gold

By The League Historian | June 20, 2026

Eleven weeks have vanished into the fog, and the Black Flag Baseball League has proven to be a treacherous stretch of water for the weak of heart. From the opening salvos in March to the sweltering heat of June, our twelve captains have clashed for every doubloon and scrap of territory. Some galleons are riding high on the swell, while others are taking on water faster than their crews can bail.

The State of the Fleet

As it stands, the Murder City Marauders have hoisted the blackest of flags, sitting atop the Head-to-Head standings with a terrifying 10-1 record. Their dominance hasn't been a matter of luck; they’ve navigated through the carnage with a nine-game winning streak that has left most of the league in their wake. Close behind, Perdomo My Last Email... holds the 2-seed, but they currently command the most valuable treasure in the sea: the lead in Total Points. With 9,015 doubloons (FPTS) in their chest, Perdomo is currently the favorite to claim the end-of-season bounty for the highest overall points.

The Heavy Hitters: Launching Cannonballs

No ship survives without firepower, and Chicks Dig The Long Ball has the biggest batteries in the league, launching a staggering 156 cannonballs (HRs) into the bleachers. However, the individual crown for hitting excellence belongs to Yordan Alvarez of the Reefside Ruffians, who has racked up 653 points. Not far behind is the rookie sensation Nick Kurtz, who has been a revelation for Chicks Dig The Long Ball with 606 points.

Over in the Perdomo camp, James Wood and Shohei Ohtani have been terrorizing opposing rotations. Wood’s 601.6 points have anchored a lineup that leads the league in walks (402 BB), proving that sometimes the best way to win a battle is to let the enemy beat themselves. Meanwhile, Bobby Witt Jr. has been the lone bright spot for the Redwater Rumrunners, leading a league-high 110 stolen base campaign with 28 of his own.

The Pitching Prowess: Throwing Daggers

While hitting wins games, pitching survives the season. Jacob Misiorowski, now of the Tidefall Thieves, has been the most feared man on the mound, carving up hitters for 702.8 total points and a 1.45 ERA. He has been the ultimate dagger-thrower in a league where pitching points are hard to come by.

Perdomo My Last Email... has built their lead on the strength of a league-best 699 strikeouts. Yoshinobu Yamamoto and Chris Sale have combined to form a terrifying duo that has kept Perdomo's pitching staff at the top of the FPTS charts (3,857.20). On the other end of the spectrum, Ganzta's Paradise has found themselves in choppy waters, allowing a league-high 94 home runs, a stat they'll need to stabilize if they hope to climb back into the playoff hunt.

The Merchant's Row: Impactful Transactions

The waiver wire has been as busy as a Tortuga port. One of the most impactful claims of the season occurred in Week 13, when G snatched up Jordan Walker after he was cast adrift. Walker has since rewarded that faith with 486 points, helping keep G firmly in the top 3. More recently, Chicks Dig The Long Ball bolstered their future by claiming Jordan Lawlar and Jasson Dominguez in June, signals that they are loading up for a deep run.

The trading block has also seen some massive shifting of cargo. The blockbuster of the season came on June 8th, when Ganzta's Paradise and the Seaglass Swashbucklers swapped major assets. Ganzta sent young stars JJ Wetherholt and Alec Bohm to Seaglass in exchange for the veteran presence of Ozzie Albies and the electric Jackson Merrill. This move was a clear "win-now" signal from Ganzta, who is desperate to bridge the gap to the 4th-place playoff line.

The Sinking Ships

It has been a season of misery for the Seaglass Swashbucklers. Despite having talented pieces, they have suffered through a nine-game losing streak that has seen them plummet to the bottom of the H2H standings at 1-10. They have been having immense trouble with choppy waters, often losing close matchups despite respectable point totals. Similarly, the Tidefall Thieves and Redwater Rumrunners find themselves three games out of the playoff picture, needing a massive second-half surge to avoid a season at the bottom of the locker.

Rest of the Season: What to Watch For

The Playoff Cutline: Only 4 teams make the postseason. Currently, Harmon (7-4) holds the final spot, but Chicks Dig The Long Ball and Smuggler's Bay are breathing down their neck. Expect every matchup between these three to be a bloodbath.
The Gold Chase: Perdomo leads the Total Points race, but Murder City and G are within striking distance. A single monster week from Ohtani or Yordan could swing the prize money.
The Trade Deadline: July 12th is the final day to exchange crew members. Watch for the bottom-tier teams to sell off veterans like Freddie Freeman or Bryce Harper to the contenders for prospect hauls.
Injury Returns: With several stars like Ronald Acuña Jr. and Munetaka Murakami sitting on the IR for various teams, their potential return (or lack thereof) will dictate who sinks and who swims in August.

Keep your eyes on the horizon and your daggers sharp. The second half of the Black Flag Baseball League season is where legends are made and scallywags are forgotten. Hoist the colors!
