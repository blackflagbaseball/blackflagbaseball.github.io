🏴 Standings Entering Week 9

Week 9 opened with a tight race at the top: Murder City Marauders and Perdomo My Last Email both sitting at 7–1, while G and Harmon rounded out the early playoff picture. With only the top four teams earning postseason berths, every cannonball fired — and every scallywag outing — was starting to matter.

⚡ Featured Matchup: Murder City Marauders vs. Perdomo My Last Email

Team Record Final Score Result
Murder City Marauders 8–1 957.1 WIN ✓
Perdomo My Last Email 7–2 715.0 Loss

This heavyweight showdown lived up to its billing. Murder City's cannons roared early and often, and once they seized the helm on Monday, they led the whole way. Bryan Reynolds (OF) posted 90.0 points and Ben Rice (C) added 85.6, while Jonathan Aranda (1B) chipped in 89.7 and Braxton Ashcraft (SP) delivered 70.8 more.

Perdomo countered with Shohei Ohtani (SP/UT) putting up a massive 92.9 points, James Wood (OF) adding 82.9, and Andy Pages (OF) contributing 68.2 — but the depth behind them couldn't keep pace with Murder City's relentless barrage. The Marauders looked every bit like a crew steering toward a top playoff seed, extending their win streak to seven.

🔥 Best Matchup of the Week: DK Barrel Blasters vs. G

Team Record Final Score Result
G 7–2 770.2 WIN ✓
DK Barrel Blasters 3–6 727.4 Loss

The closest battle of Week 9. G led from Monday onward, but DK kept the pressure on all week. Ronald Acuña Jr. (OF) was an absolute weapon for DK with 95.9 points — the highest-scoring hitter in the league this week — supported by Freddie Freeman (1B) at 47.5 and Oneil Cruz (OF) at 58.7.

G's balanced attack proved just enough to survive. JJ Bleday (OF) put up 75.0, Michael Harris II (OF) added 64.5, CJ Abrams (SS) contributed 53.2, and Cristopher Sanchez (SP) delivered 54.2 to seal the narrow victory.

Redwater Rumrunners 557.0 – Seaglass Swashbucklers 480.4

Redwater never trailed, riding steady offense from Bobby Witt Jr. (SS) (30.6 pts), Samuel Basallo (C) (50.3 pts), and Chase Meidroth (UT) (43.6 pts). Seaglass got a historic outing from Jacob Misiorowski (SP) — a league-high 124.6 points — but the rest of the roster fell quiet and couldn't make up the gap.

Chicks Dig The Long Ball 610.0 – Tidefall Thieves 489.4

Tidefall held the early advantage, but Chicks stormed back over the weekend behind Kody Clemens (2B) (45.7 pts), Casey Schmitt (2B/MI) (38.7 pts), and Nick Kurtz (UT) (42.3 pts). Pitching from Michael Wacha (SP) (44.7 pts) and Nick Martinez (SP) (41.0 pts) sealed the comeback win.

Smuggler's Bay Skullcrushers 472.2 – Reefside Ruffians 390.5

Smuggler's Bay led wire-to-wire thanks to Hunter Goodman (C) (35.1 pts), Keider Montero (SP) (47.2 pts), and Grant Taylor (RP) (44.4 pts). Reefside's hopes sank with Sandy Alcantara (SP) posting –28.0 points — a scallywag performance that didn't flip the matchup but certainly didn't help.

Harmon 697.9 – Ganzta's Paradise 446.9

Harmon's rotation dominated from the jump: Luis Castillo (SP) (62.2 pts) and Gerrit Cole (SP) (58.9 pts) set the tone, while Colson Montgomery (3B/SS) (60.9 pts) and Pete Crow-Armstrong (OF) (44.9 pts) fueled the offense. Ganzta was sunk by Tanner Bibee (SP) (–54.1 pts) — though even benching him wouldn't have flipped the result.
